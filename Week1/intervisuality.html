Referentiality & Intervisuality in Films

This webpage will explore how different films accross time and regions reference from each other visually to build conversation. References between films could happen in various forms, the reference to mise-en-scene (costumes, props, sets), to cinematography (image composition, camera angles and movements), to editing and sound, etc. Since this is particularly about the visuality of scenes across different films, therefore it circulates a form of inter-visuality, in parallel to intertextuality, between films.

La Pointe Courte (1955)

La Pointe Courte is a black & white 35mm film made by the Belgium-born French director Agnès Varda. "It has been cited by many critics as a forerunner of the French New Wave, with the historian Georges Sadoul calling it 'truly the first film of the nouvelle vague.' The film was shot and takes place in Sète in the south of France, 'La Pointe Courte' ('short point') being a tiny quarter of the town, known as the fisherman's village" (qtd from wiki).

Persona (1966)

Persona is a black & white 35mm film made by the Swedish director by Ingmar Bergman. "The story revolves around a young nurse named Alma (Bibi Andersson) and her patient, well-known stage actress Elisabet Vogler (Liv Ullmann), who has suddenly stopped speaking. They move to a cottage, where Alma cares for Elisabet, confides in her, and begins having trouble distinguishing herself from her patient" (qtd from wiki).

Ghost in the Shell (1995)

Ghost in the Shell is animated film (using both hand-drawn celluloid animation and digital computer-generated techniques) made by the Japanese director Mamoru Oshii. "The film is set in 2029 in the fictional New Port City and follows Major Motoko Kusanagi, a cyborg public-security agent who hunts an enigmatic hacker/ghost known as "the Puppet Master". The narrative incorporates philosophical themes that focus on self and identity in a technologically advanced world. The music, composed by Kenji Kawai, includes vocals in classical Japanese" (qtd from wiki).
